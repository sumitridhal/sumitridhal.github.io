This note walks **two** transitions from [Object & Archive](https://objectandarchive.com/).

## First beat: curtain + split

A **tinted full-bleed layer** lifts off the diptych—**Étude de forêt** on the left, **Monet detail** on the right—while the **wordmark stays on top** of both the panel and the photography, with a light gradient scrim so the SVG stays legible.

### Layer model (curtain)

1. **Split media** — two halves, `object-fit: cover`, full-bleed in the stage (`inset: 0`).
2. **Curtain** — same box as the stage, muted sage gradient (`--writing-oa-hero-curtain`); `transform: translateY(-100%)` when open.
3. **Wordmark overlay** — highest z-index, bottom-centered `oa-white.svg`, soft dark gradient scrim behind it for contrast on both the curtain and the paintings.

Replay runs the same sequence without reloading the page. Reduced-motion visitors skip the tween and see the revealed layout immediately.

## Second beat: left-column carousel handoff

On the live homepage the **left half** stacks a full-bleed plate with a smaller, sharper “card” on top. Advancing the strip **expands the inset** while the **backdrop and inset zoom in on the same timing**; then the **background crossfades** to Pale Face and the **next plate and field zoom in together** as **Amphitrite** replaces the card (here: **Pale Face** → **Amphitrite** over a **Pale Face** field, with **Étude de forêt** as the initial backdrop). The right half stays a static Monet detail so this embed stays readable in a narrow column.

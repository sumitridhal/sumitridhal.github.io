**Invader fractals** (see Tarbell’s [Invader Fractal](http://levitated.net/daily/levInvaderFractal.html) write-up on Levitated) are a **tiny generative grammar**: pick a random **3×5** block of on/off cells, then **mirror** the outer columns across the middle so the composite reads as a **5×5** invader. The combinatorics stay toy-like, yet the results scan instantly as arcade silhouettes.

## Half grid, full symmetry

Store only `half[x][y]` for `x ∈ {0,1,2}` and `y ∈ {0,…,4}`. For the full column `X ∈ {0,…,4}`, map `x = min(X, 4 − X)` and copy `half[x][y]`. That is the whole trick: **one reflection** replaces hand-tiling the right side of every row.

## Bit budget and legibility

Because the grid is symmetric, you only ever author **15 bits** per character. The full **5×5** is a presentation choice: the same half seed could be drawn at one pixel per cell on a status LED grid or blown up as chunky squares in print. Tarbell’s larger pieces treat those glyphs as **tiles** in a bigger composition—each invader stays cheap to regenerate while the field reads as dense texture.

Two colours (ink and paper) are usually enough: symmetry already carries most of the “alien” read, so palettes stay disciplined unless you deliberately push poster noise.
